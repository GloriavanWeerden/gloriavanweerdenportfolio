Integrative Conservation Clinic

The Integrative Conservation Clinic is a website where conservation specialists can access region-specific resources to inform research, connect with others in their field and build up a personal library to create new interdisciplinary connections.

The final goal of this project was to create and compile a complete branding guide for ourselves. This included creating a logo, selecting colour schemes, and general rules of use. These were to be presented in an InDesign file and submited as a clickable PDF. If you would like to see this finished product, you can check it out using the button below.

Background

Dr. Sabo came to our program with a project her team had already tried to begin. They had wanted to build an open-source site, that allowed conservation professionals to share papers, projects, and real world solutions. The initial solution they first showed to us was bulky and difficult to understand.

Formative Research

We began seeking out conservation specialist for interviews. Pooling the teams resources and connections, we recruited a wide variety of practitioners, including:

  • Forestry Technicians
  • Wildlife Outreach Professionals
  • Conservation Education Specialists
  • Aquatic Biologists
  • Environmental Conservationists
  • Geo-statistical Consultant
  • Conservation Educators

Through several rounds of research and testing, we worked to find our best solution. Our first phase was a lesson in managing client expectations and learning to recognize when you're heading in the wrong direction.
